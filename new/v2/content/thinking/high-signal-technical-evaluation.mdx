## Why this framework exists
When I review projects, I am not optimizing for polished demos. I am optimizing for evidence that the builder can scope ambiguity, make tradeoffs, and ship reliable systems.

## The 5 signals I prioritize
- Problem clarity: Can they explain the real problem, users, constraints, and success criteria?
- Architecture quality: Is the system decomposition intentional, observable, and resilient to common failures?
- Execution discipline: Are implementation choices coherent, testable, and maintainable under iteration pressure?
- Measurable outcomes: Can they prove impact with metrics, not only screenshots and claims?
- Learning velocity: Do they document mistakes and show iteration loops with improved decisions?

## What separates strong projects
Strong projects include concrete tradeoff discussion, failure handling strategy, and measurable outcomes. Weak projects tend to stop at feature lists and UI walkthroughs without explaining reliability or scalability decisions.

## How this applies to recruiting
For technical interviews and portfolio review, this framework lets me quickly identify candidates who can contribute in production environments where correctness, delivery speed, and ownership all matter.
